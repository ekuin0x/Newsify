Lewis Hamilton slams Helmut Marko as Sergio Perez breaks silence on offensive F1 slur

knowledia 2023-09-15 12:04:42
Source page
Red Bull F1 adviser Marko was widely criticised for referring to Perez as "South American" and suggesting it makes him mentally inferior to his European counterparts Sergio Perez said he has accepted Helmut Marko's apology for his public slur about his nationality but Lewis Hamilton feels it is not enough. The long-serving Red Bull adviser sparked widespread criticism for his comments about the racer being "South American". Of course, as Perez is from Mexico, that is factually wrong. But the truly offensive part of what he said was his suggestion that where he comes from makes him mentally weaker than his European rivals. After initially appearing to double down on what he said, Marko later issued an apology through Red Bull-owned Servus TV. "I would like to apologise for my offensive remark and want to make it absolutely clear that I do not believe that we can generalise about the people from any country, any race, any ethnicity," his statement read. "I was trying to make a point that Checo has fluctuated in his performance this year, but it was wrong to attribute this to his cultural heritage." Perez was silent publicly on the matter until he was asked about it by reporters ahead of this weekend's Singapore Grand Prix. In response, he revealed he had also received a private apology from the Austrian and that, for him, the matter was closed.
