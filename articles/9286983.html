HRT is commonly used to manage menopause-related symptoms in women, such as hot flashes, mood swings, and vaginal dryness. It can also be prescribed for transgender individuals to facilitate gender transition by aligning secondary sexual characteristics with their gender identity. In men, HRT is less common but may be recommended to address conditions like hypogonadism, where the body does not produce enough testosterone. HRT for men can help improve muscle mass, bone density, and overall well-being. HRT can be administered in various forms, including pills, patches, creams, gels, and injections, with the choice depending on the patient's specific needs and preferences.
