Opinions expressed are those of the author. | Membership (fee-based) Sep 13, 2023, 07:15am EDT | Share to Facebook Share to Twitter Share to Linkedin Gyehyon is a Venture Capital Founder, CEO & President of MVLASF , a forum for successful executives & entrepreneurs, and a published author. getty A new social dilemma arose from the pandemic when it comes to tips for service workers. In a period of inflation, adding tips to every food service interaction has come to be known as Tipflation. I am a fan of tip culture in general, as it shows gratitude for those in the service industry who work to support the local economy, often with a smile on their face and a positive attitude. What tip makes the real respected gratitude signal? Many employees in food service and other service jobs where gratuity is anticipated are starting out in the workforce. They're 16-year-olds with new driver's licenses looking to cover gas and a lifestyle apart from their parents. This is a generalization, of course, but it helps to identify at least one section of those in the service industry. With teens representing one part of the service worker demographic, you can also assume that they are working within their community, perhaps for a family business or a family friend's business. They encounter family, friends, classmates and others in their social circle throughout their work. With the strain of the pandemic impacting the service industry, supporting these businesses and those who worked in them was a no-brainer. Customers, often neighbors, agreed collectively to increase tips in many cases, even as inflation continued. Many historical restaurants, bistros, museums, libraries, parks and cafes even found outright donations to help keep them afloat. However, if the collective consensus to support tip culture falters, customers can feel as though they are overburdened or forced to take over the obligation to provide wages to employees from the business owner. This kind of breakdown could stem from a number of sources, including the perception that the business owner is not acknowledged or respected by the local community. My theory is that tips are multiplied by the original service chargewhich is already inflated. For example, if the service charge for your meal was $100 but is now $120, a previous tip of 10% goes from $10 to $12. If the tip is not 10% but instead 20%, this turns $10 into $24, which is more than double. Increasing the tip rate was based on community-favored action to sustain the local neighborhood economy during the pandemic, but inflation was not yet a factor when this collective action began. Finding Favorable Tip Practices A favorable practice could take the pulse of your customers to determine the best course of action for tips. For example, itd be great to make anonymous surveys to get real feedback from your visitors by the business development team of each service provider as below: Do you think Tipflation (tip inflation increasing present points from 5%15% in per-pandemic and now 15%25% in post-pandemic) makes people hesitate to get service, and they go to local businesses area with less frequently? Y/N. For each industry, including SMEs and startup platforms, the tip format may or may not apply depending on the country, as the practice of tipping is not necessarily standard in the global culture. Surprisingly, many countries haven't had tip etiquette at all, even for the taxi business, and you can see the interesting stories and diversity of cultural backgrounds . Sometimes, the tip would be an effective solution to overcome the hurdle, but each country has different tax regulations for the income and market perception. Tips shouldnt be a travel tax. When I went to restaurants 10 years ago, if I paid a tip of 20%, the waiter/waitress remembered me and my family or business colleagues, and often chefs or managers visited our tables. However, even though I pay 25% tips now, it feels like an obligation rather than the previous 10 years ago when it was based on favorable gratitude that came from respect for the individual services. If service tips become normal, more people will go to restaurants and use more taxis and Ubers so that the turnover will increase. This kind of social shift turns back the gentle tone and manners with favorable local characteristics and cultural experience in neighborhoods to make sustainable communities grow in the long view because local neighborhoods visit their neighborhood business owners who are next to you more frequently. The Ultimate Tip Platform And Culture Eventually, community consensus is important for the tip charging rate. It'd be great if the business development team leaders reviewed the conventional tips innovatively. The leaders can decide whether it makes sense to remove tips and increase wages and incentives for their employees. They may need to increase the unit prices in the short term, but they can stabilize the operation. The increased price can help push up the size of the sale if the operation has been stable because both sales unit price and table turnover would help make more revenue. They would have higher table turnover as customers are motivated by the savings from tipping the staff, and employees would be motivated by the wages and incentives that come with a high level of performance. In addition, if tips remain a part of a business, some tips can be cut for the neighbors if they show proof that they live in the same town. This would help support a museum or library as local residents are likely to visit more often due to their proximity. It'd be great if the beautiful and reasonable tip cultures would come back home sweet home again with a little help from surveys and making changes that benefit both the employee and the customer.
