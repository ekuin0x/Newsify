Inside 'ghost town' housing estate for NHS staff now run by drug dealers and vandals

knowledia 2023-09-11 11:41:33
Source page
It was once home to NHS lifesavers and dedicated health workers, but now drug dealers and vandals run riot. Locals say they feel unsafe living near the once-beloved 'ghost town' It used to be a bustling community of NHS hospital workers and their families, but now the derelict housing estate has been "left to rot" by the council after being abandoned three years ago. Remaining residents near Hospital Close, in Evington, Leicester, have described the area as a "ghost town" and said it has become a magnet for vandals, drug dealers and fly-tippers since 2019. Locals living in the shadows of Leicester General Hospital have shared their fears and sadness, and said they feel unsafe in their own homes since it became a hotspot for antisocial behaviour. Ashley Lewis, 32, who lives on a neighbouring road, explained: "It's quite spooky to be honest, it's a complete ghost town now. It used to be a thriving little community of NHS staff and their families." Image: Tom Maddick / SWNS) Tom Maddick / SWNS) When the University Hospitals of Leicester NHS Trust decided it could not afford to maintain the homes or upgrade them to modern standards three years ago, eviction notices were issued to health workers - including many nurses - living in the properties, and they were forced to move out. Now, those left living behind nearby say they have been "forgotten about". Ashley continued: "It's gone from having these angels and life-savers to drug dealers and thugs.
