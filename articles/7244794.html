Holly, 42 , released a heartfelt tribute to the vacuum-loving teen after his death was announced in a statement released by ITV, praising the "delightful young man". Holly commented on This Morning's Instagram post writing that Matty was a "talented young man". She said: "Just so unbelievably sad. Such a lovely and talented young man sending my love to his friends and family, this must be such a difficult time." Read More Related Articles Phillip Schofield and wife 'on good terms' after therapy over This Morning affair Read More Related Articles Holly Willoughby is 'a bag of nerves' amid career comeback following Phillip Schofield scandal This Morning released a statement on Sunday afternoon announcing the sad news. They wrote: "Everyone at This Morning is shocked and saddened to hear of the passing of Matty Lock, aged just 19. He was a delightful young man, a joy to work with and his passion for vacuum cleaners and general mechanics was infectious. "His family came to the studio and they were so proud that hed turned a childhood hobby into a career. We will miss him and send our condolences to his family." Holly paid tribute to Matty on Instagram (Image: Instagram) Matty was a popular member of the Labour Party in Liverpool's Sefton and Merseyside and was recently elected as a town councillor in Maghull. Labour North West's account added: "I want to express our deepest condolences at the loss of Cllr Matty Lock. He had his whole life ahead of him, with huge potential, who I know was admired and loved across our party. Our thoughts are with his mum and dad and family at this difficult time." Matty Lock pictured with Angela Rayner, who has led tributes after his sudden death Matty became an internet sensation with his honest reviews of new vacuums and appeared on the ITV show multiple times over the years to discuss his passion. For his 13th birthday Matthew even went to the Numatic factory in Somerset where he went home with two incredibly rare Henry Hoovers to add to his collection. He first discovered he had a knack for fixing hoovers while at his friend's grandma's house when he fixed her broken Henry which she was about to throw out. "Some of the Henrys I have are 40 years old. I like to fix them but if they're beyond repair I will make a good one out of two hoovers," he said. "My collection is mostly Henrys and Dysons. I've got original versions which are made out of metal and Bakelite. My collection is well into the hundreds now. They are all in my workshop in my garden which is where I do all my vacuum repairs."
