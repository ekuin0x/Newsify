ESPN teases new Monday Night Football theme song with Chris Stapleton, Snoop Dogg and Cindy Blackman - which will air for the first time TONIGHT ESPN's new theme song for Monday Night Football is a cover of a rock hit The network has Monday Night Football rights through 2033 DailyMail.com provides all the latest international sports news By Jake Nisse For Dailymail.Com Published: 16:57, 18 September 2023 | Updated: 16:57, 18 September 2023 e-mail 1 View comments ESPN has teased a new Monday Night Football theme song featuring Chris Stapleton , Snoop Dogg and drummer Cindy Blackman Santana. In the 30-second clip, Stapleton can be heard singing Phil Collins ' 'In the Air Tonight' as Blackman performs the song's famous drum solo. Snoop Dogg does not rap or speak in the clip, but can be seen posing dramatically with his arms spread open. At the end of the clip, it is revealed that the song will premiere tonight and be a precursor to Monday Night Football games all season. Chris Stapleton did a cover of Phil Collins' 'In the Air Tonight' for Monday Night Football Snoop Dogg is also involved in the theme song, though he wasn't heard in the teaser And drummer Cindy Blackman Santana performed the song's famous drum solo as well Unlike usual, there are actually two Monday night games this week. The Panthers will host the Saints at 7:15ET while the Steelers will host the Browns at 8:15ET. The first game will appear on ESPN while the latter contest will be shown on sister network ABC. Last week, Monday Night Football debuted with the Jets' win over the Bills at MetLife Stadium. ESPN signed a new deal for Monday Night Football in 2021, extending its broadcast rights through 2033.
