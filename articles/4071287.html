Norwich ace Jonathan Rowe is bouncing up the Championship table and eyeing promotion leap

knowledia 2023-09-15 12:04:42
Source page
Rowe's five goals in six games for the Canaries earned him a call-up to the England Under-20 camp during the international break and recognition as EFL Young Player of the Month From his trampoline in the back garden as a kid, Jonny Rowe could almost jump as high as the rainbow arch next door at Wembley stadium. Now he is leading Norwich City's promising bid to bounce back into the Premier League. Rowe's five goals in six games for the Canaries earned him a call-up to the England Under-20 camp during the international break and recognition as EFL Young Player of the Month. Check out his slick opener against Millwall, or a soaring finish into the top corner against Hull, and you'll understand why the 20-year-old winger has a spring in his step. And if Norwich are promoted through the play-offs, he will only have to nip round the corner to celebrate at the house where he learned to fly. Rowe said: "Growing up, I could see the Wembley arch from my back garden - and once we got a trampoline (to get an even better view of it). We lived close enough to hear the roars and the atmosphere whenever a goal went in, even from inside the house. We were so close there was only a railway track between us and the stadium." Most players never make it to Wembley in a lifetime Rowe played there every week, in AFC Wembley's youth set-up, until he was snapped up by Norwich. It was a good piece of business.
