Strictly's Krishnan Guru-Murthy reveals 'there will be a defibrillator' on hand while he performs after doctors warned he could 'drop dead' due to a genetic heart condition By Bethan Edwards For Mailonline Published: 11:40 BST, 13 September 2023 | Updated: 12:23 BST, 13 September 2023 e-mail 5 View comments Krishnan Guru-Murthy has revealed that Strictly Come Dancing bosses will have a defibrillator on set while he performs after he was diagnosed with a genetic heart condition. The Channel 4 newsreader, 53, has hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, which has killed two of his cousins and it might prevent his progress on the hit BBC show. He has said that it is important that he 'keeps his heart out of the red zone' and his cardiologist could not promised 'he won't drop dead' while performing. Krishnan told The Sun : 'The basic advice is, I've got to keep my heart out of the red zone, which is the last 15 per cent of your heart rate. 'So, I've got to keep my heart rate below about 140, and I don't know whether you can do that or not, in a 90-second very fast dance. The Charleston will be a challenge, but we'll see.' It means that the BBC will have a defibrillator on standby in case of any emergencies. He added: 'I have to listen to my body. I have that condition Elvis had and footballers have when they can suddenly drop. The task for me is more about training and not raising my heart level for longer periods of time than a two-minute dance. There will be a defibrillator for me. I have a heart condition but it doesn't mean you can't do Strictly.' Krishnan has the full backing of his wife Lisa who encouraged his decision to get out his comfort zone and hit the dancefloor. Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy causes the wall of the heart chamber to thicken, meaning they can become stiff and therefore reducing the amount of blood taken in and pumped out to the body with each heartbeat. After covering the likes of the pandemic and Ukraine war, Krishnan went on to explain that his 'stressful and very intense' job was what pushed him to do something fun like Strictly. The latest celebrities to take on the dancing competition were recently teamed up with their new professional partners and have started training.
