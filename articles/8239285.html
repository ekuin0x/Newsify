Senior Tories accuse Rishi Sunak of being weak on China after arrest of suspected spy

knowledia 2023-09-11 00:04:42
Source page
Prime Minister has refused to describe Beijing as a 'threat' despite increasing Conservative pressure Senior Tories have attacked Rishi Sunak's stance towards China following the revelation that a parliamentary researcher was arrested on suspicion of spying for Beijing. The Prime Minister has steadfastly refused to refer to the state as a "threat" since entering No 10 and has consistently championed maintaining a dialogue with China. But the news that two men, including one who had penetrated the heart of Parliament, have been arrested for allegedly breaking the Official Secrets Act, has prompted leading Conservatives to demand Mr Sunak take a tougher line. Prominent China-sceptic Sir Iain Duncan Smith told i that Parliament "needs to have tighter security", before adding: "We need to refer to China as a systemic threat as Sunak did before he was elected and changed his policy. China see us as being weak."
