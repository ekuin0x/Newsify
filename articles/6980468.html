Recession fears as UK economy shrinks by more than expected in July - with strikes & rain behind the decline

thesouthernreporter 2023-09-13 17:10:43
Source page
The UK economy shrank by 0.5% in July which was worse than analysts expected, prompting more recession fears. The UK economy shrank more than expected in July, according to fresh data from the . It said the decline was driven by strike action by teachers and NHS workers which caused a fall in output from the services sector. This years wash-out summer also had a part to play, hitting the construction and retail industries and causing the economy to contract by 0.5 per cent. The figures were worse than analysts had previously forecasted and have been released amid fears of a recession as households and businesses continue to grapple with high inflation rates.
