A Cheeky 'Space Trick' Reveals A Cosmic 'Wild Flower' In Deep Space (Photo)

in_mashable 2023-09-14 10:03:11
Source page
The universe is like a giant garden, and we've got proof! The European Southern Observatory (ESO) used their super-duper telescope (VLT) to take a picture of a faraway galaxy. It looks like there are four bright blue flower petals around it, but here's the cool part: it's not actually flowers. The big galaxy in the middle is like a cosmic magnifying glass bending and making the light from another hidden galaxy in the background look like flowers. Mind-blowing right? Well, that's what's happening here. Because of the special space trick, we see two copies of the hidden galaxy from Earth and that's why it looks like there's a ring of flowery petals around the orange galaxy in the middle. SEE ALSO: The picture shows galaxies from way back when the universe was just a baby, and they're hanging out in the Hercules constellation. To take this awesome picture, scientists used a special tool called the Multi Unit Spectroscopic Explorer (MUSE) on ESO's super-telescope in Chile. The ESO's Very Large Telescope (VLT) is like a super-duper space spyglass and it's one of the fanciest ones on the planet! You can find it hanging out at the Paranal Observatory in the super-dry and super-high Atacama Desert in Chile. This spot is like the VIP section for stargazing because it's so high up and free from city lights making it perfect for cosmic detective work Cover Image: ESO
