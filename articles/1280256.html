MADD Canada to unveil monument honouring victims of impaired drivers in Ontario

torontosun 2023-09-08 18:21:13
Source page
MADD Canada is unveiling a new memorial monument in Ontario dedicated to victims of impaired driving at Bramptons Chinguacousy Park on Saturday The Ontario monument will have the names of 137 victims of impaired drivers engraved on it and will join other provinces with a monument paying tribute to the innocent victims of impairment A ceremony marking the event will take place at 11 am with approximately 250 family members and friends whose loved ones are memorialized expected to attend Dignitaries expected to speak at the ceremony include Brampton Deputy Mayor Harkirat Singh Brampton Centre MPP Charmaine Williams MADD Canada National President JaymieLyne Hancock and representatives of police fire and emergency services They will be gathering at the parks Memorial Plazacenotaph area near the Central Park Dr and Queen St E corner of the park The ceremony will include a candlelight vigil for the victims followed by the first public viewing of the monument MADD Canada has other provincial memorial monuments dedicated to victims of impaired drivers in Newfoundland and Labrador New Brunswick Nova Scotia Manitoba Saskatchewan Alberta and Quebec
